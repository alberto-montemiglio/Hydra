Water Intake Detection for Dementia patients

This project aims at tackling the issue of dehydration in dementia patients, one of the leading causes of acute confusional states in dementia patients. This is a novel, non-intrusive method of detecting water intake by means of a throat microphone and a Multi-Layer Perceptron Classifier algorithm. This website is aimed at dementia patients and carers and provides the collected raw data, the data analysis and a progress bar of how much water should be drank in a day. The water intake is tailored to the age and weight of the patient using the data gathered in the login phase. Finally, as a reminder to drink more if the current water intake is lower than what should have been drank since the start of the day, an actuation platform consisting of a peristaltic water pump pours water in a glass.
